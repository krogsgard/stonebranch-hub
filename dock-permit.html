Alabama Power Non-Residential Permit (NRP)

Required for any commercial dock/shoreline construction on Lake Martin (FERC-licensed reservoir). Four phases: Phase 1 (initial review) → Phase 2 (agency consultation) → Phase 3 (FERC review, 6–12 months) → Phase 4 (construction, 2 years allowed). No construction may begin until FERC issues its order AND Alabama Power issues the permit. Must disclose ALL future development plans — APC will not entertain multiple applications for a single development.

Key Compliance Rules — Lake Martin

Structure Limits & Setbacks

Setback from Property Line
25 feet minimum
From extension of property line into the lake. All waterfront structures.
Max Size per 100' Shoreline
1,000 sq ft
Floating dock, boathouse, wet slip. Walkways ≤6' wide excluded.
Max Length
150' or 1/4 water width
Whichever is less. Including moored vessels.
Min Spacing Between Docks
50 feet
Between multiple docks on the same property.
Boat Ramp Width
20' maximum
May extend "reasonable distance" from shoreline (APC discretion).
Narrow Slough Rule
No more than 1/4 across
No structure may extend over 1/4 of the way across (incl. moored vessels) at full pool.
Full Pool Elevation
491' MSL (490' Martin Datum)
Lake Martin summer level. Fee ownership at same elevation.
Control Strip
30' from 491' MSL
AL Power easement where applicable.

Prohibited & Restricted

Prohibited on Fee/Easement Land
Pools, spas, hot tubs
In-ground or above-ground. No exceptions.
Covered Overwater Structures
Case-by-case approval
Must be documented in application. No guarantee of approval.
Flotation Material
Encased/closed-cell only
Must be marine-grade. Must not waterlog or sink when punctured.
Bank Stabilization
Riprap preferred
Seawalls case-by-case. Seawalls >1,000' need USACE pre-approval. Riprap required at toe.
