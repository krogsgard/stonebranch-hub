Brand Positioning

Archetype: The Caretaker/Sanctuary. Not flashy, not influencer bait — considered, intentional, high-quality.

Tone: Calm, rooted, unhurried.

“The water’s been here for 70 years. We’ll be here for a while too.”

“97 acres of Alabama forest, one creek, no noise. Bring the people worth it.”

Target Audiences (priority order)

  1. Group retreats / gatherings (2–4 cabins) — corporate retreats, family reunions, friend weekends, church retreats. Highest-revenue, hardest-to-serve segment that nobody owns in this market.
  2. Couples seeking nature-premium stays — $450–650/night is accessible for 30s–50s couples.
  3. Event anchors — weddings and larger events come Phase 2+ once capacity and operations are in place.
  4. Extended retreats (4–7 nights) — writers, remote workers, families on week-long lake trips.

Key Differentiators

  • Purpose-built, not someone’s lake house being rented out
  • Unified brand and quality across all cabins
  • The land itself: 97 acres, creek corridor, wooded/rolling
  • Group-focused (multiple cabins can be booked together)
  • OZ/mission story is interesting but don’t lead with it publicly — investor/press story, not guest story

2 — Booking & Revenue

For a new property with no reviews and no brand recognition, OTA-first is the right call. Direct bookings come after you’ve accumulated reviews and brand awareness.

AirbnbLargest audience, guest trust, insurance, payment infra3% host fee, guests pay 14–16% service fee3%
VRBOStrong family/group audienceAnnual sub or 8% per-booking fee5–8%

Google Vacation RentalsFree to list via PMSRequires booking engine to connectFree
Direct (your site)Zero commission, guest data, repeat relationshipsRequires established brand + SEOStripe ~2.9%

Phase 1: Launch on Airbnb + VRBO simultaneously via a channel manager (OwnerRez or Hospitable).
Phase 2: Build direct booking engine after 20+ Airbnb reviews. Offer 5–10% discount for direct bookings.

Suggested pricing structure

  • Base rates: $450/2BR, $650/3BR
  • Peak summer premium: +20–30%
  • Weekend premium: +15% year-round
  • Multi-cabin retreat discount: −5–10% when booking 2+ cabins together
  • Minimum stays: 2 nights standard, 3 nights on summer weekends, 5–7 nights July 4 week

Cleaning / Turnover

  • Turno (Phase 1) — auto-schedules from Airbnb/VRBO bookings, photo checklists, cleaner marketplace. First property free.
  • ResortCleaning (8+ cabins) — full operations platform, $5–7/property/month.
  • Turnover cost: ~$100–200/cabin for 2–3BR in Lake Martin area.
  • Pro tip: Identify cleaning team before you open. In rural markets, cleaning is the operational bottleneck.

Review Management

  • Automate the ask — Hospitable/OwnerRez send post-checkout review requests automatically.
  • Respond to every review publicly — even 5-star.
  • Target 10 Airbnb reviews in first 3 months — search ranking threshold.
  • Mid-stay check-in catches problems before they become review issues.

Key risk: If permits slip by more than 6 weeks from mid-March, you’re looking at December CO, which is uncomfortably close to the deadline. Track weekly.

OZ operational requirement: Under Vulcan OZB, LLC must be the active operating business generating income from the OZ property (≥50% gross income from active business in the OZ). Passive rental income could jeopardize OZB status — work with OZ counsel to ensure the business structure is correctly categorized as “active” hospitality.
